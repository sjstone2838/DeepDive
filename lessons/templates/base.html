Study

We founded DeepDive on the belief that you learn best when you’re constantly problem-solving. No kicking back and dozing off as you click through dozens of videos – with DeepDive, you’ll be answering questions, working through examples, and getting real-time feedback from the start!

Get started by trying out a few courses. Learn more about an existing passion or find a new one - courses cover a broad, ever-expanding range of topics. Work on your own schedule - time commitments per course range from a few hours to multiple weeks. Pay attention to the feedback you get along the way to ensure you pass your tests and keep on unlocking new content!

Teach

Have a subject you want to educate the world about? Have a specific group of classmates, employees, or friends you want to teach? Want to verify that students are actually learning your material and easily measure their progress? And you want to do all that for free? DeepDive is for you!

Use our creator tools to create courses with multiple modules. Fill your modules with a rich set of content – text, videos, documents, quizzes, etc. End each module with a test to gauge your students’ performance and ensure students unlock new material only once they’ve mastered what comes before it.

Analyze

Track aggregated student progress at a summary level to identify stumbling blocks and refine your curriculum. Drill down into individual performance all the way to the question level. Understand course enrollment – who are your students and where do they come from?

Courses

Self-explanatory - a curriculum for a student to learn about a topic. Courses require a name, a genre, and a description - they can also include an image or logo

Modules

Modules are the units that make up courses. You can think of them as "course sections" or, for a shorter course, individual lessons. Modules are unlocked by students one after another. So when a student enrolls in a course, he or she will only have access to the first module, but will be able to access all of the elements in that module.

Module Elements

Module elements are the "pages" or "slides" that students will see within a module. We don't like to call them "pages" or "slides" because there is a lot more you can do with a module element than a traditional slide or PDF. In addition to text, tables, and images, you can embed videos and attach documents (including templates for students to fill in). You also can - and should - include questions to provide students with real-time feedback as they progress through the elements of your modules! You can select from 3 kinds of module element:

  • Content: include text, pictures, video, and documents
  • Quiz: all the functionality of a Content element, plus questions. Answers are not saved and students can change and submit answers as many times as they like. Multiple quizzes can be embedded throughout a module
  • Test: similar to a Quiz, but used as a gating mechanism at the end of a module. Test answers are logged and students unlock access to the next module only if they beat a predefined score. Each module must include one and only one test, as its last element.
Questions

DeepDive currently supports two types of questions:

  • Form: open-response
  • Radio: select one from a multiple choice list. Radio questions can have as many answer choices as desired, but only one answer can be correct
